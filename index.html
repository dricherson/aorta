The Matt Crowder Memorial Andover Bowl is a post season junior football tournament open to all Mid-States junior football teams grades 3 through 6. This year's bowl will be held on November 2nd, 3rd and 4th in Andover Kansas.
All teams will play at least 2 games. All players will receive a custom medal for participating. Additional awards will be awarded to each player on the first and second place teams. The head coach on each team will also receive a team trophy for first and second place. The head coaches from each team in the championship game will award an MVP trophy to a player on the opposing team. Referees and bowl organizers will select one player from each championship game to receive the coveted sportsmanship award. This award is given to a player who showed good sportsmanship througout the game to both their team mates and the opposing team.
8 teams will be selected to play under the lights on opening night, Friday, November 2nd*. *Schedule subject to change based on field availability.
Hot food and cold drinks will be available for sale at each of the event locations.
Purchase an event pass wrist band and get into every game, all weekend, for free.

Coaching
It was through tears that Matt Crowder called home that autumn evening and said, "Dad, they called me 'coach'." It had been his dream. So it was not a surprise that Sunday afternoon in November 2005, when he called home again - this time with hardly a voice left from yelling - to say, "Mom, we WON!!! I am standing on the football field with my team. We won the Newton Bowl! We are undefeated! I love you, mom."
 
Today, you have honored our son, Matt by your participation in this tournament. We cannot think of a more fitting tribute. Thank you.
 
To the players: Coach Crowder would tell you to give it all you've got. You can do it. Don't stop now. Be the best you can be. Don't let anyone tell you that you are too little, too short, or too slow...You can do it. You can do it in football...and you can do it in life.
 
To the coaches: You set the stage for a lifetime of achievement. Every child deserves a chance. Every child needs encouragement. Every child needs someone to look up to. Don't take it lightly that you might be that "someone".
 
To the parents: Your children are a gift. Treasure each day. Never miss an opportunity for a kiss, a hug, a laugh, or an "I love you".
 
"Train a child in the way he should go,
and when he is old he will not turn from it."
Proverbs 22:6
 
To Matt's teachers and coaches through the years: Thanks for being patient with our high-energy son. Thanks for letting him play, even though he was too little, too short, too verbal, etc. Thanks for finding the best in him and encouraging and equipping him to succeed in business, as well as in life.
 
To Matt's friends (and there are so many): Your friendship is so unique. What started out as a 7th grade team has turned into lifelong friends. Matt Crowder was blessed to have such friends. Thank you.
 
To the Andover community: How do we say 'thanks' for the support you have been to our family during this time? You have shared memories, brought meals, sent cards, called, hugged, laughed and cried with us. And while we weren't even aware that we needed those things, you have literally carried us through this time. Thank You, Thank You. Thank You.
 
With our heartfelt appreciation

Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy
Matt suffered from a type of heart disease known as Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (abbreviated HCM). You may not have heard of this type of heart disease. However, about 1 in 500 people are afflicted with HCM. HCM is predominantly a genetic disease, but many cases are not genetically linked. HCM is a disease that affects the heart muscle. The most consistent feature is excessive thickening of the walls of the heart muscle, primarily the septum. Some people call this enlarged heart. HCM is one of the silent type diseases. With no family history to help the physician, a typical health physical will not identify the disease. An echocardiogram (ultrasound of the heart) is normally required to identify HCM. With early detection a person can have a normal productive life. In Matt's case, there was no detection.
Some Statistics:

1 in 110 births (AHA statistic).

7,000 to 14,000 young people die from

Sudden Cardiac Death every YEAR (CDC statistic).

36% of these deaths are directly attributed to HCM.

1 in 500 people of the general population have HCM;

Scholarship Information
The original intent of this bowl was to raise money primarily for the Andover Junior Football program as well as the other Andover school football programs. We have established a memorial scholarship in Matt's name.
A scholarship has been established up for 2 high school seniors at each of the Andover high schools. A fixed amount will be donated per recipient towards their college education. We will select candidates who submit an application based on their good standing (no bad grades or other disciplinary issues), community service and volunteer status with the annual Andover Bowl.
All funding for scholarships will be taken from the proceeds generated at the annual bowl event.

Andover Junior Sports Association - Mission Statement
The Andover Junior Sports Association's (AJSA) mission is to improve youth football programs in the city of Andover, Kansas. We do this by raising funds for the Andover Junior Football program, the Andover Middle School program and the Andover High School program. Our fund raising activities are directed at increasing the awareness and support for football in the Andover community.
The junior football program receives funds from our organization that are used to buy practice equipment, helmet decals and provide for an end of the year banquet for each of the players. In the event the City of Andover does not have the ability to provide a scholarship for a player who can't afford to play, AJSA will provide funds to help them meet the costs. The senior high and middle schools each receive an equal donation of equipment for use in their programs. In the past, this has included helmets, chin straps and other safety related equipment needed by the players. In addition, AJSA provides 2 scholarships to each high school for senior athletes who are in good standing and are planning on enrolling in college in the following year.
AJSA promotes community involvement and awareness by holding an annual junior football tournament. This event brings in over 2,500 junior football players, spectators and local volunteers. The event is held each year in honor of a former junior football coach who passed away. The community is encouraged to come out and watch the junior football players compete in a 2-3 day tournament that culminates in a championship game for each grade level.
